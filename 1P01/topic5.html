DEMOGRAPHIC

Finland’s official languages are Finnish and Swedish, though there are minority groups of Lapp- and Russian speakers. Those who speak Finnish dominate the population at a staggering 93.5%, whereas those who speak Swedish only make up 6.3% of the population. The religious groups in Finland are Evangelical Lutheran, Eastern Orthodox, and other minority religions. Similarly to the languages, one religion dominates the Finnish population. Evangelical Lutheran sits at 97% of the population, Eastern Orthodox at 1.2% of the population, and 1.8% other minority religions (Cousoukis, Finland People 1990, 2003).
